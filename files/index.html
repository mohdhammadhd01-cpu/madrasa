Miracles & Signs in Islam

Below are some well-known miracles and signs associated with Islam and the Prophethood of Muhammad (peace be upon him). These are presented as commonly taught examples for students and visitors.

  • The Qur'ān: Considered by Muslims to be a lasting miracle — unique in its language, guidance, and preservation over 1400+ years.
  • Preservation of the Qur'ān: The Qur'ān has been preserved in its original Arabic text and widely memorized (Hifz) across generations.
  • Isra & Mi‘rāj: The night journey and ascension of the Prophet (peace be upon him), a significant event described in the Qur'ān and Hadith.
  • Splitting of the Moon (Shaqq al-Qamar): A miracle reported in early Islamic sources.
  • Scientific & Moral Guidance: Many students study the Qur'ān and Hadith to find moral and natural signs that encourage reflection and learning.

These are presented for study and reflection; teachers at Jamia Imamuddin Madrasa provide contextual lessons in classes.

Khulafā’ al-Rāshidīn (Rightly-Guided Caliphs)

Traditionally, the Khulafā’ al-Rāshidīn are four leaders who followed the Prophet’s teachings after his passing. Below are their names and short notes.

  1. Hazrat Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (رضي الله عنه) — The first Caliph, a close companion of the Prophet (peace be upon him).
  2. Hazrat Umar ibn al-Khattāb (رضي الله عنه) — The second Caliph, known for justice and administrative reforms.
  3. Hazrat Uthmān ibn Affān (رضي الله عنه) — The third Caliph, under whose leadership the Qur'ān was compiled in the standardized text.
  4. Hazrat Ali ibn Abi Ṭālib (رضي الله عنه) — The fourth Caliph, cousin and son-in-law of the Prophet, noted for scholarship and piety.

Note: historically the term "Khulafā’ al-Rāshidīn" refers to these four. If you meant another list of five names for a local/national context, tell me which one and I will add it.

Ahādith & Basic Duʿāʼs

Selected Ahādith (short)

  • Arabic: إِنَّمَا الأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ
    Transliteration: "Innamal a'malu bin-niyāt"
    English: "Actions are judged by intentions." — (Sahih al-Bukhari)
  • Arabic: لَا يُؤْمِنُ أَحَدُكُمْ حَتَّى يُحِبَّ لأَخِيهِ مَا يُحِبُّ لِنَفْسِهِ
    Transliteration: "La yu'minu ahadukum hatta yuhibba li akhihi ma yuhibbu li nafsihi"
    English: "None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself." — (Sahih Muslim)

Basic Duʿāʼs & Daily Remembrances

  • Before eating
    Arabic: بِسْمِ اللَّهِ
    Transliteration: Bismillah
    English: "In the name of Allah."
  • After eating
    Arabic: الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ
    Transliteration: Alhamdulillah
    English: "All praise is due to Allah."
  • Short remembrance
    Arabic: سُبْحَانَ اللَّهِ، الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ، اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ< br/> Transliteration: SubhanAllah, Alhamdulillah, Allahu Akbar
    English: "Glory be to Allah, All praise is due to Allah, Allah is the Greatest."
  • For seeking knowledge
    Arabic: رَبِّ زِدْنِي عِلْمًا
    Transliteration: Rabbi zidni 'ilman
    English: "My Lord, increase me in knowledge." (Qur'an 20:114)
  • For parents
    Arabic: رَبِّ ارْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِي صَغِيرًا
    Transliteration: Rabbi irhamhuma kama rabbayani saghira
    English: "My Lord, have mercy upon them as they brought me up when I was small." (Qur'an 17:24)

Teachers at Jamia Imamuddin Madrasa will explain these Ahādith and Duʿāʼs in class, with context, pronunciation, and source details.
